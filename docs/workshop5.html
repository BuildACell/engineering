WORKING GROUPS

In addition to the below listed groups, we welcome suggestions for in situ formation of new working groups after the general discussion.

(4) Impact, policy, funding, milestones group
Beginner's guide to Build-a-Cell
For people who are not part of the community yet, also for non-researchers, funders, journalists
How can we unify the field? What challenges will the synthetic cell field face: funding, research policy, biosafety and biosecurity
Milestones, points of concern (running through the previously composed milestones paper draft will be good primer for non-specialists)
Lead: kate
google doc link best group

(1) Interlab Group
A list of potential components/subsystems that have already been demonstrated in isolation by differently and could be potentially integrated together in a "synthetic cell” to demonstrate some interesting function. Output is diagram showing the components and some made-up graphs showing what we think we could demonstrate.
A list of individuals/groups who can produce models for the components/subsystem and one or more groups who agree to develop a simulation of the integrated system, establish design guidelines for experiments to follow, and predict the results of those experiments. Target date for first output from the model is 3 months.
A list of individuals/groups who will produce experimental constructs (DNA sequences and/or DNA provided under OpenMTA) for each of the components/subsystems. Components should be made available to anyone who is interested within the next 3 months.
A list of individuals/groups will will receive the constructs and attempt to integrate them into a synthetic cell performing some interesting function, measure characteristics that correspond to the modeling/prediction results. Target date for first attempt is 6 months.
Lead: Richard
